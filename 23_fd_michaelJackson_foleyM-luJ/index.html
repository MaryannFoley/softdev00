    XY Grid

    Grids are containers that allow you to control padding and placement of sections of content on your page.


    half cell with padding
    half cell with padding 
    
    <div class="grid-x grid-padding-x">
        <div class="cell small-6">
            <div class="callout">
                half cell with padding
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="cell small-6">
            <div class="callout">
                half cell with padding
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>


    Above, you can see many important aspects of the XY grid. One is that there is a limit to 12 total for the values in each of the cells (the 6s in the example above). You can also see that above we have padding on. Let's try an example with different numbers and no padding...


    1/12 cell
    6/12 cell
    3/12 cell
    2/12 cell 
    
    <div class="grid-x">
        <div class="cell small-1">
            <div class="callout">1/12 cell
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="cell small-6">
            <div class="callout">6/12 cell
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="cell small-3">
            <div class="callout">3/12 cell
            </div>
        </div>
        <div class="cell small-2">
            <div class="callout">2/12 cell
            </div>
        </div>
    </div>

    Buttons

    With Foundation, you are able to create buttons of many styles without the hastle of making them look nice. This is written as follows: 


    
    <a type="button" class="button XXXX">Title</a>

    The different styles are displayed below with their names (what would replace XXXX in the code above).

    primary secondary success alert warning
    Other button styles

    Note: These can all be used with all above styles, primary is being used as an example.

    Outline buttons
    
            <a type="button" class="button hollow primary">Primary</a>

    Primary
    Disabled buttons
    
                <a type="button" class="button primary" disabled>Title</a>

    Title

    Tables

    Foundation provides tools to make tables easily customizable.

    Table Header Table Header
    Content Goes Here Mom always taught us to curl up in a ball and remain motionless when confronted. Pound is tic-tac-toe right?
    Content Goes Here You mean the guy we're meeting with can't even grow his own hair? Come on! It's a wonderful restaurant! You boys know how to shovel coal? Excuse me, do these effectively hide my thunder? 
    
    <table class="hover">
      <thead>
        <tr>
          <th>Table Header</th>
          <th>Table Header</th>
        </tr>
      </thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Content Goes Here</td>
          <td>Mom always taught us to curl up in a ball and remain motionless when confronted. Pound is tic-tac-toe right?</td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Content Goes Here</td>
          <td>You mean the guy we're meeting with can't even grow his own hair? Come on! It's a wonderful restaurant! You boys know how to shovel coal? Excuse me, do these effectively hide my thunder?</td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>

    The table is automatically striped (add the class "unstriped" to the table tag if you don't want stripes). The hover class has been added, try hovering over the table to see this in action.

    Callouts

    Callout culture? Nope! Just some good looking panels and alerta!

    This is a primary callout

    Annhog's coming? Let me out that Queen. And THAT'S why you always leave a note.

    
    <div class="callout primary">
        <h5>This is a primary callout</h5>
        <p>Annhog's coming? Let me out that Queen. And THAT'S why you always leave a note.</p>
    </div>

    Any of the styles for the buttons above can be used for callouts.
